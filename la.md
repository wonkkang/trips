## Parking Guide (minivan)
A minivan fits every normal LA garage (clearance is usually 6'6"-8'2"; a minivan is ~5'10"). Only three habits matter: skip stalls marked **"COMPACT"**, favor structures over street parallel parking when the van is full of people, and read the full sign pole before leaving it on any street — LA stacks 3-4 rules per pole (permit zones, street cleaning, rush-hour tow-away).

| Area / Day | How parking works | Cost ballpark |
|---|---|---|
| **Airbnb (Hancock Park)** | Ask the host: is there a driveway spot, and are the surrounding streets overnight-permit-restricted? Much of Hancock Park is permit parking at night — the driveway answer decides everything. | Free if driveway |
| **Santa Monica (Day 1)** | Best move: Downtown Santa Monica city structures 1-8 around the Promenade — **first 90 min free**, easy walk to pier. Beach lots next to the pier are flat-rate and fill early on July Saturdays. | Structures ~$1-3/add'l hr; beach lots ~$15-20 flat |
| **Hollywood Walk of Fame (Day 2)** | Park once at **Ovation Hollywood garage** (enter on Highland or Orange) — validated with purchase, elevator up to TCL/Walk of Fame. Avoid the hawker "event" lots on the Blvd. | ~$3-10 with validation |
| **Rodeo Drive (Day 2)** | Beverly Hills city garages are the hidden deal: **first 1-2 hours free** (e.g., 461 N Bedford Dr, 9510 Brighton Way — both a block from Rodeo). | Free-cheap |
| **Koreatown (Day 2/3)** | Nearly every restaurant/mall has its own lot: Koreatown Plaza and Madang garages validate; many restaurants share small valet lots. Street parking exists but signs are brutal — default to the business's lot/valet. | Free-validated; valet ~$3-6 |
| **Griffith Observatory (Sun night)** | Pay-by-plate ~$10/hr near the top; cheaper to park partway down Western Canyon Rd and walk up 10-15 min. Evenings are busy — circle patiently. | ~$10/hr top; less below |
| **Melrose / Fairfax / La Brea shops** | Metered street parking, mostly 1-2 hr; Melrose Trading Post (Sunday) has paid lots nearby. Pay meters by app (ParkMobile/LA Express Park). | ~$1-2/hr meters |
| **Old Pasadena / Olive Young (Day 3)** | Pasadena city structures ring Colorado Blvd (Schoolhouse at 33 E Green St is closest) — **first 90 min free**. Arriving at 9:45 AM for the 10 AM Olive Young opening = easy spot. | Free 90 min, ~$2-3/hr after |
| **The Grove (Day 3 flex)** | On-site garage, first hour free, validation with purchases; Farmers Market surface lot with validation. | ~Free-$10 |
| **DTLA (Day 3 flex)** | Grand Central Market validates at nearby garages; otherwise use posted-rate surface lots ($10-15 flat). Don't street park downtown. | ~$10-15 flat |
| **UCLA (Day 4)** | Visitor pay-by-plate in the numbered structures — all-day max ~$15-17. For the noon tour, arrive by ~11:15 and use the structure named in your tour confirmation (often Structure 2, off Hilgard). Van height is fine in all UCLA structures. | ~$15-17/day |
| **Westfield Century City (Day 4)** | Huge garage, first hour free, app/validation discounts; tall clearance. | ~Free-$10 |
| **Getty Center (Day 4)** | One big official garage, per-car flat rate (~$25, cheaper after 3 PM); museum itself is free. Tram up from the garage. | ~$25 flat |
| **Restaurant valets** | Very common at sit-down LA restaurants (Mother Wolf, Bestia, etc.). Minivans are totally normal for valets. Keep $5-10 cash tips handy. | ~$10-15 + tip |

**Rules of thumb:** park once per neighborhood and walk the cluster; photograph the parking sign and your level/row; never leave visible bags in the van (standard LA advice); prices drift, so trust the posted rate over this table.

## Day 1 - Saturday: Arrival + Santa Monica

### Suggested Flow
- 11:00 AM land at LAX → rental car (budget ~45-60 min for shuttle + counter)
- Airbnb check-in is likely mid-afternoon, so go **LAX → Santa Monica first** (only 20-30 min north) with lunch there
- Late afternoon: check in at the Airbnb, drop bags
- Evening: dinner near Larchmont, or stay in Santa Monica through sunset (~8 PM in July) and dinner there

### Places to Visit (pick by energy level)
1. **Santa Monica Pier + Pacific Park**
   - Classic SoCal in one frame: ferris wheel, arcade, ocean air. Zero learning curve after a travel day.
   - Time: 1.5-3 hours
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Santa+Monica+Pier" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a> | <a href="https://santamonicapier.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Official Site</a>
2. **Third Street Promenade + Santa Monica Place**
   - Pedestrian shopping/street-performer strip two blocks from the pier; everyone can split off and regroup.
   - Time: 1-2 hours
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Third+Street+Promenade+Santa+Monica" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a>
3. **Palisades Park (bluff walk)**
   - Free palm-lined clifftop path above the beach — the best easy photo walk in Santa Monica, great at golden hour.
   - Time: 30-45 min
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Palisades+Park+Santa+Monica" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a>
4. **Venice Beach Boardwalk** (optional add-on)
   - Raw, weird, creative beach culture — murals, skaters, muscle beach. Very different vibe from polished Santa Monica.
   - Time: 1-1.5 hours (bike or drive 10 min south)
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Venice+Beach+Boardwalk" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a>
5. **Venice Canals** (optional, quiet)
   - Hidden walkable canal neighborhood 5 min from the boardwalk; calm and photogenic.
   - Time: 30-45 min
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Venice+Canals+Walkway" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a>
6. **Beach bike ride (The Strand)** (active option)
   - Rent bikes near the pier and ride the beach path toward Venice — the single most fun way to cover both.
   - Time: 1.5-2 hours
   - Maps: <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Marvin+Braude+Bike+Trail+Santa+Monica" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Open in Google Maps</a>

## Taco Truck Tracker (must-do)
Trucks move and set up mostly evenings (~5-6 PM until late). Confirm the pin on Google Maps day-of — search the truck name and check "open now."

1. **Leo's Tacos Truck** — The al pastor benchmark: trompo spit with pineapple flicked into the taco. Multiple trucks; the 1515 S La Brea Ave location (Mid-City) is ~10 min from the Airbnb. <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Leo's+Tacos+Truck+1515+S+La+Brea" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Maps</a> | <a href="https://www.leostacostruck.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Site</a>
2. **Angel's Tijuana Tacos** — TJ-style: mesquite-grilled asada, adobada, handmade tortillas. Several locations/stands around LA; check maps for the nearest that night. <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Angel's+Tijuana+Tacos" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Maps</a>
3. **El Flamin Taco** (Koreatown) — Ktown's own al pastor gold standard, charred on the trompo; evenings on Ktown corners. Easiest to combine with your Ktown nights. <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Flamin+Taco+Koreatown" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Maps</a>
4. **Tacos El Chino** (Koreatown area) — TJ-style charcoal-grilled tacos on handmade tortillas at street prices. <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Tacos+El+Chino+Los+Angeles" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Maps</a>
5. **Kogi BBQ Truck** — *Korean-Mexican fusion.* Roy Choi's short-rib taco truck started the modern food-truck movement in 2008 — a fitting fusion pick for a Koreatown-heavy trip. Runs Tue-Sat ~11 AM-11 PM at rotating stops; check the daily schedule on their site. <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Kogi+BBQ+Truck" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Maps</a> | <a href="https://kogibbq.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Site</a>

Ordering tips: cash is fastest, order al pastor first, say "con todo" (onions/cilantro/salsa), and grab a consommé if they have birria.
